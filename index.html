Hello! I’m Brett.

Welcome to my little place on the internet. I’m a data product manager, analyst, teacher and writer from El Segundo, CA.

I’ve been doing this technology entrepreneurship and data analysis thing for a while now. So far, I had the privilege to:

  1. Perform data analysis for the Department of Defense, and the University of California
  2. Help $500M and start up CEOs evaluate and launch new business ventures
  3. Be a part of the inaugural class of Master's of Technology Management program at UCSB

With this site I'm looking to:

  • Help developers, analysts, and product managers learn data science with articles I write.
  • Produce in-depth courses like Data Analyis for Beginners, Predictive Analytics for Product Managers and Machine Learning for Developers with Python amongst others.

Many people think you need a Math or CS degree before you can become a great Data Scientist.

I have neither. I came from a Political Philosophy background.

I only started this Data Science thing at the age of 25 because I desperately needed some skills for creating a startup. Having only marketing skills wasn’t enough. After 10 months of self-taught data science and development education, I found myself working with competition winnning data science techniques with ease.

That’s crazy!

Yeah. I know. Before I started, I thought data science was insanely hard. It’s something I’ll never be able to get good at even if I died trying. Likewise for programing. But this story just proves that you’re much better than you believe you are.

  • Think you can’t become a good developer? Think again.
  • Think you can’t become a good data scientist? Think again.
  • Think you can’t make a living freelancing? Think again.

But of course, there are real challenges to become good at something and make a living out of it. I can help you if you’re interested.
